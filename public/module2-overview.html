About This Module

Module 2 follows directly from Module 1 (Ignite Your Engine) and shifts the focus from athletic foundations to deliberate skill development. Each student chooses one skill to focus on at Week 1 — and tracks that same skill all the way through to a coach-assessed showcase at Week 8.


The program is delivered through Power2ADAPT's My Athletic Journey (MAJ) digital platform. Every session has three parts: Learn (read a short concept), Do (complete the session challenge), and Reflect (write a personal reflection). Students must complete all three parts to progress to the next week and earn their XP.

Weekly Breakdown
Week 1 What is Skill?
Learning Concept
Skill is Repeating a Movement Correctly Under Pressure
This module is about developing skills as an athlete. Skill is not about being talented — it is about being deliberate. The most skilled athletes got there through focused repetition, not accident.
  • A skill is any movement you can perform reliably and accurately, even when tired or under pressure.
  • Skills are built through deliberate, focused practice — quality repetition beats quantity every time.
  • Every athlete has strengths and areas to develop. Both matter.
  • This module: choose one skill to focus on and build it across 8 weeks.
⚡ Session Challenge

Choose Your Skill — Set Your Goal

  1. Discuss skill options with coach (throwing, catching, footwork, jumping, sport-specific)
  2. Choose one skill and write it down
  3. Agree with coach on what success looks like at Week 8
  4. Complete 15 quality reps of chosen skill
  5. Rate current skill level out of 10 (baseline)
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"What skill do I most want to improve — and why does it matter to me?"


Students connect their skill choice to the sports and activities they love.

Week 2 Repetition 🔁 Rep Champion Badge
Learning Concept
Why Repetition Builds Skill — The Brain-Body Connection
When you repeat a movement over and over, your brain builds a physical pathway — like a trail through a forest. The more you walk the trail, the clearer it becomes.
  • The brain learns movement through repetition — every rep strengthens the signal between brain and muscles.
  • Quality matters more than quantity: 20 focused reps beat 100 lazy ones every time.
  • Mistakes are part of the process — the brain learns from errors as long as you pay attention to them.
⚡ Session Challenge

20 Quality Repetitions Challenge

  1. Remind yourself what good technique looks like
  2. Complete 20 reps, one at a time — no rushing
  3. Check in after every 5 reps: still correct?
  4. Slow down if technique breaks — don't repeat errors
  5. Rate quality out of 10 after 20 reps
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"What did you notice about your movement after 20 focused repetitions?"


Did it start to feel more automatic? What will you focus on fixing next week?

Week 3 Footwork
Learning Concept
Footwork is the Foundation of All Athletic Skill
Every skill in sport starts with your feet. Where your feet are, how fast they move, and how they connect with the ground determines almost everything else your body can do.
  • Good footwork puts you in the right position to execute your skill cleanly.
  • Poor footwork forces the upper body to compensate — creating errors and injury risk.
  • Fast feet come from light contact with the ground — think quick, not heavy.
⚡ Session Challenge

Footwork Ladder or Cone Drill Time Trial

  1. Walk through the pattern slowly first
  2. Run through 3 times — focus on foot placement, not speed
  3. Coach records time on 4th (full-speed) run
  4. Complete 3 more runs trying to improve time
  5. Note fastest clean time — revisited in Week 7
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"How does your footwork affect everything else you do as an athlete?"


Think about a moment when feet were in a bad position — what happened to the rest of the movement?

Week 4 Throw & Catch
Learning Concept
How Accuracy and Timing Work Together
Throwing and catching develop hand-eye coordination, timing, and communication. Even if a student's sport doesn't involve throwing, the coordination it builds transfers to everything.
  • Accuracy in throwing comes from a consistent release point and follow-through every time.
  • Catching starts with your eyes — track the object all the way into your hands.
  • Communication between passer and receiver makes both athletes better.
⚡ Session Challenge

20 Consecutive Catches — Partner Challenge

  1. Start 3 metres apart
  2. Count every clean catch aloud together
  3. When you drop, reset and go again without frustration
  4. When 20 is reached, each take one step back and repeat
  5. Record longest streak and furthest distance
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"What did your partner do that helped you catch better — and what did you do to help them?"


Quality of throws, communication, encouragement — how did working together change performance?

Week 5 Agility 💨 Agility Ace Badge
Learning Concept
Agility — Changing Direction Without Losing Speed
Agility is the ability to change direction quickly and under control — without losing speed or balance. It is one of the most valued qualities in sport and it is highly trainable.
  • True agility is reactive — responding to what's happening, not just running a set pattern.
  • Good deceleration is essential — you have to slow down to change direction fast.
  • Low body position and bent knees provide a stable base to push off from.
⚡ Session Challenge

Agility Course — Time Trial

  1. Walk through the course to learn the pattern
  2. Complete at 70% speed — focus on clean direction changes
  3. Coach records time on first full-speed attempt
  4. Complete 3 more attempts — improve time without losing control
  5. Record best time (retested in Week 7)
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"What slowed you down on the agility course — and what is your plan to fix it?"


Body position? Foot placement on turns? Acceleration out of the change? Be honest and specific.

Week 6 Skill Under Pressure
Learning Concept
Why Skills Break Down When You Are Tired or Competing
Under pressure, the brain reverts to older, less refined movement patterns. Physical fatigue and mental pressure both disrupt smooth movement — which is why practising under pressure is essential.
  • Physical fatigue makes technique harder to maintain — the body takes shortcuts when tired.
  • Mental pressure creates tension that disrupts smooth movement execution.
  • Focusing on process (technique) rather than outcome helps skill hold up under pressure.
⚡ Session Challenge

Skill Under Fatigue — Repeat Week 2 After a Warm-Up Circuit

  1. Complete the full warm-up circuit at strong effort (deliberate fatigue)
  2. Immediately attempt 20 quality reps of chosen skill
  3. Notice how technique feels different when tired
  4. Apply the same focus and cues used when fresh
  5. Rate quality out of 10 — compare to Week 2 rating
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"Was your skill better or worse when you were tired — and what did you do to keep going?"


What strategy helped maintain technique? What fell apart first? What held up best?

Week 7 Game Situation
Learning Concept
Applying Your Skill in a Team Game Context
Skills trained in isolation need to be tested in a game. A game introduces unpredictability, teammates, opponents, and decisions — all of which make executing your skill harder and more meaningful.
  • Game situations require reading what's happening and then executing your skill.
  • Decision-making speed is part of skill in a game — the right skill at the wrong time is still an error.
  • Mistakes in games are normal. What matters is your response — reset and go again.
⚡ Session Challenge

Game Activity — Skill in Action

  1. Before the game, remind yourself of your skill focus
  2. During the game, look for moments to use your skill consciously
  3. After each round, check: Did I use my skill? Was it effective?
  4. Ask a teammate: Did you notice me using my skill?
  5. Share one successful moment with your coach
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"When in the game did you use your skill well — and what made that moment possible?"


Positioning, decision-making, timing — what set them up to execute successfully in a live situation?

Week 8 Skill Showcase — Capstone 🏆 Module 2 Master Badge
Learning Concept
Showing What You Have Learned
This is the Module 2 capstone. Eight weeks ago students chose a skill, rated themselves, and started building. Today they demonstrate what they have developed — with their coach assessing progress against the Week 1 baseline.
  • Skill development is a process, not an event — 8 weeks of deliberate practice compounds.
  • A showcase is not about perfection — it is about demonstrating honest progress and the habits that created it.
  • The skills built this module are permanent. They don't go away.
⚡ Session Challenge (Coach-Assessed)

Skill Demonstration — vs. Week 1 Baseline

  1. Warm up specifically for the chosen skill
  2. Complete 20 quality reps with coach observing
  3. Rate skill level out of 10 — compare to Week 1 rating
  4. Ask coach: "What is the biggest technical improvement you've seen?"
  5. Set one skill goal to continue into Module 3
✏️ Reflection Prompt

"How has this skill changed for me over 8 weeks — and what made the difference?"


What habits, cues, and moments created the improvement? What will they keep doing?

Awarded by coach upon successful skill demonstration at Week 8 capstone. Worth 50 XP.

How the Digital Platform Works

Students access My Athletic Journey (MAJ) on their phone or tablet before, during, or after each Power2ADAPT session. The app guides them through the three-step weekly structure and automatically unlocks the next week once all three steps are complete.


Coaches can view each athlete's progress, submit skill assessments, and leave feedback notes through the coach login. Badges are awarded in-app and visible on the student's profile.
