This week's concept
The Difference Between Strength and Explosive Power
Strength and power are related — but they're not the same thing. Understanding the difference will change how you train.
Strength is how much force your muscles can produce. Power is how fast you can produce that force.
An athlete who can jump high and explode off the mark has power — even if they don't look "strong" in the traditional sense.
Power training focuses on explosive movements: jumping, bounding, and quick changes of direction.
Every time you jump and land well, you're training your body to produce and absorb power safely.
Soft, controlled landings protect your joints. Always land with bent knees and quiet feet.
Power is the combination of strength and speed. Build both, and your athletic ability jumps to a new level.

Your challenge this week
Power Up — Squat Jump Challenge
This challenge is about quality explosive movement. You'll complete 10 squat jumps. The goal is not height — it's landing well and jumping again immediately.
1
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower into a squat position.
2
Explode up as high as you can, swinging your arms to help.
3
Land softly with bent knees — feet should be quiet on impact.
4
Immediately drop into your squat again and repeat for 10 reps.
5
Your coach will watch your landing. Once you nail it, add height.
Change it up
Can you do 10 clean squat jumps with your hands on your hips (no arm swing)? Much harder — and great for leg power.
