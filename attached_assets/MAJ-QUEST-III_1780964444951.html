Q3 · Week 1
What is Focus?
The Challenger begins by understanding what focus actually is -- and realising it can be trained.
35 XP
Concept
Focus is a Trainable Skill
Focus is directing attention to what matters most, right now. Most athletes have never been taught how to focus. Distractions are everywhere -- focus is choosing what to pay attention to despite them. Attention is trainable.
Do
Mindful Warm-Up Challenge
Coach leads a 2-minute quiet focus routine before training begins. During the session, notice:
  • 3 moments when your attention fully locks in
  • 3 moments when it drifts away
  • Share observations with coach at end of session
Reflect
Your Starting Point
"What makes it hard for me to focus during training -- and when is my focus at its best?"
Q3 · Week 2
Breathing and Calm
The Challenger discovers the one tool they already carry -- and learns to use it deliberately.
35 XP
Concept
Box Breathing
Breathing is the only automatic function you can also control consciously. Shallow, fast breathing signals danger to the brain. Box breathing uses equal counts to create calm and control: in for 4, hold for 4, out for 4. Simple, effective, and available to you anywhere.
Do
Box Breathing Before Your Hardest Activity
  • Stand still, relax shoulders
  • Breathe in through nose -- 4 counts
  • Hold -- 4 counts
  • Breathe out through mouth -- 4 counts
  • Repeat twice, then go straight into the activity
  • Use as a reset tool after mistakes
Reflect
In Control
"Did breathing help you feel more in control -- and how did it change your performance?"
Q3 · Week 3
Self-Talk
The Challenger learns that the voice in their head is not random -- it is trainable.
35 XP
Concept
Choose Words That Help
Negative self-talk creates tension and reduces performance. Positive self-talk is not pretending -- it is choosing words that help. Cue words are short, powerful phrases for key moments: Drive. Relax. Reset. Trust it. Elite athletes use self-talk deliberately -- it is trained, not accidental.
Do
Build Your Self-Talk Toolkit
  • Write 3 encouraging phrases for when training gets hard
  • Choose one cue word for today
  • Use it out loud (quietly) at least 5 times during the session
  • After mistakes -- use self-talk instead of going silent or negative
Reflect
Words Under Pressure
"What did you say to yourself when things got tough today -- and did it help?"
Q3 · Week 4
Attention Control
The Challenger faces their greatest enemy -- their own mind under pressure.
60 XP
🌬️ Calm Under Pressure · +25 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Process Over Outcome
Task focus = what you are doing right now. Outcome focus = what might happen as a result. Outcome focus during execution creates anxiety and disrupts automatic movement. The cue: "What do I need to do RIGHT NOW?"
Do
Task Focus Drill
  • Choose one activity as your task focus activity
  • Identify the specific task cue: arm drive, soft landing, eyes on target
  • Every time the mind goes to score or outcome, bring it back to the task cue
  • Rate your task focus out of 10 at the end
Reflect
Process vs Outcome
"What happened to your performance when you focused only on the task -- not the outcome?"
Q3 · Week 5
Pressure and Nerves
The Challenger discovers that nerves are not the enemy -- they are the signal.
35 XP
Concept
Nerves Are Performance Tools
Nerves = body getting ready to perform. Increased heart rate, alert senses, and adrenaline are all performance tools. The feeling of nerves is almost identical to excitement -- you can choose the label. Trying to make nerves disappear usually makes them worse. Repeated pressure exposure builds confidence.
Do
Pre-Performance Routine
Design a 30-second pre-performance routine: breathing + cue word + one physical reset (roll shoulders, shake hands).
  • Use it before every turn in the competition
  • Notice how you feel first turn vs. last turn
Reflect
Settle and Perform
"How did you feel before the challenge -- and what helped you settle and perform?"
Q3 · Week 6
Consistency
The Challenger learns that consistency is not talent -- it is focus, applied again and again.
60 XP
📊 Consistent Performer · +25 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Consistent Focus Compounds
Consistent performance comes from consistent focus, not talent. It is easy to focus when fresh and excited -- the real skill is focusing when tired or struggling. Building a pre-session routine equals mental consistency. Consistent focus compounds over time.
Do
Full Session Focus and Effort Log
  • Set a focus and effort target before the session starts
  • After each activity: rate effort/10 and focus/10 quickly
  • At end: identify highest and lowest focus moments
  • Show log to coach and discuss one insight
Reflect
Peak Focus Moments
"When was your focus highest this session -- and what was different at those moments?"
Q3 · Week 7
Focus in a Team
The Challenger realises that focus is not just personal -- it is contagious.
35 XP
Concept
Your Energy is Contagious
Your energy and attention are contagious. A focused athlete raises the standard around them. Team focus is built through shared purpose. The most focused teams communicate more, reset faster, and maintain higher intensity for longer.
Do
Team Relay Focus Agreement
  • Before the relay: team agrees on one shared focus cue (e.g. "drive hard off the mark")
  • Say it together before each race
  • If focus drops, one teammate calls the cue to re-engage
  • After relay: discuss as a team when focus helped and when it was lost
Reflect
Collective Focus
"How did our team's collective focus change the result -- and what was your role in maintaining it?"
Q3 · Week 8 · Capstone
My Focus Toolkit
The Challenger claims their mental toolkit. The tools are theirs to keep forever.
85 XP
🏆 Module 3 Master · +50 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Your Toolkit is Permanent
Every athlete builds a unique combination of tools that works for them. Breathing, self-talk, and task focus are now yours. The skill is making them automatic -- using them without thinking when it matters most.
Do
Full Session Using Your Complete Toolkit
  • Before session: write your 3 tools and when you plan to use each
  • Use breathing, cue word, and task focus deliberately -- especially under pressure and after mistakes
  • After each activity: note -- did I use my tools?
  • Tell coach your 3 tools with one real example of each
Reflect
Your Level 3 Legacy
"What are the 3 focus tools I will keep using beyond this module -- and why do they work for me?"
