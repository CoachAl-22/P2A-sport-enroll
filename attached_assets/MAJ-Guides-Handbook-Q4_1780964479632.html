THE GUIDE'S HANDBOOK

Quest IV: Rise Above

Quest IV is the end of Level 1 and the beginning of everything else. Your job is to help athletes find their leadership identity, practise it under pressure, and leave with a legacy they are proud of.

Section 1

The Quest IV Coaching Shift

A Word on Quest IV

In Quest III you coached what you could only infer. In Quest IV you are coaching something even harder to see -- who someone is becoming, not just what they are doing.

Leadership is caught, not taught. Athletes learn to lead by watching you lead. They learn to encourage by being encouraged. They learn to handle mistakes by watching you handle yours.

Your job in Quest IV is to see the leader in each athlete before they can see it themselves -- and name it.

👁️
See It Before They Do

Identify leadership moments as they happen. "That was a leadership decision just then. Did you notice?"

📢
Name It Publicly

Private acknowledgement grows one athlete. Public acknowledgement grows the whole group.

💬
Encourager Is Coaching

The Encourager badge is not participation. It requires coach observation of specific, genuine encouragement behaviour.

🏆

Section 2b

Coaching the Legacy Conversation

👑 Coaching the Legacy Athlete Conversation

This is a dedicated protocol for the Week 8 sit-down. The most important coaching conversation of Level 1.

How to Prepare

  • Review the athlete's Week 1 self-leadership commitment
  • Recall one specific moment from each of Q1, Q2, Q3 that you observed in this athlete
  • Identify their leadership identity (did they stay true to their Week 1 choice?)
  • Prepare one specific growth statement: "The thing I noticed change in you across Level 1 is..."

How to Run It -- 5 Minutes

  1. Start with the full journey: "You've done 32 weeks. Think back to Quest I. Who were you then?"
  2. Name what you observed across all four Quests -- be specific, be honest
  3. Ask: "What are you most proud of?" -- wait. Listen fully.
  4. Ask: "What do you want to build on in Level 2?" -- their answer, not yours
  5. Present the Legacy Athlete badge. Name what it represents for this specific athlete.

What to Say When Presenting the Badge

Not This

"You've worked hard and earned this."

Say This

"I am giving you this badge because [specific observation from across Level 1]. That is what Legacy means."

Session Cards

👑 Quest IV -- Rise Above Realm of Leadership · The Hero
Q4 · W1
What is Leadership?
35 XP
What to Notice

How each athlete frames leadership. Who sees themselves as a leader already? Who sees leadership as something that belongs to someone else? Both responses are important data. The athlete who says "I'm not really a leader" is often the one most ready to grow.

Before

Tell athletes this Quest is about leading -- not being the fastest or the best. Share one specific leadership moment you have personally witnessed from someone in this group.

During

When athletes share their self-leadership commitment, listen for specificity. "I'll try harder" is not a commitment. "I will arrive 5 minutes early and be ready before coach calls us in" is. Help athletes make it specific.

After

Confirm app complete. Reflection prompt. Note each athlete's chosen leadership identity and commitment -- these inform your coaching conversations for the next 8 weeks.

Q4 · W2 💬 Encourager
Being a Great Teammate
65 XP
What to Notice

Who encourages naturally and who stays silent. Both are important. The silent athlete is not necessarily a poor teammate -- they may be a lead-by-example leader. But for the Encourager badge, verbal and specific encouragement is the standard.

Before

Know exactly what specific encouragement looks like vs. generic praise. "Good job" is not specific. "You held your technique through that whole set even when you were tired -- that's what we're training" is specific.

During

Count specific encouragements from each athlete. If an athlete has delivered three genuine, specific encouragements, they are on track for the badge. If they have not engaged at all, prompt once: "Find something real to say to [name] right now."

After

Award Encourager where criteria met -- sticker ritual. Confirm badge in app. Reflection prompt.

Q4 · W3
Communication
35 XP
What to Notice

Body language as communication. Watch for athletes whose physical presence says "I don't want to be here" -- slumped shoulders, averted eyes, disconnected body. This is communication. Address it directly but privately: "Your body is telling your team something right now -- what do you want it to say?"

Before

Prepare the relay with a clear standard: silent handover = team runs. Brief athletes on the rule before starting.

During

Enforce the standard without exception. The first silent handover is a teaching moment. Stop the relay: "What was the standard? What happened? Go again." The consistency is the lesson.

After

Confirm app complete. Reflection prompt. Ask: "Which communication -- verbal or body language -- was harder to control today?"

Q4 · W4
Handling Mistakes
35 XP
What to Notice

The 10 seconds after a mistake. This is the coaching window. Who resets quickly? Who stays in the mistake? Who affects others with their response? The athlete who resets visibly is a leadership example -- name it in the moment.

Before

Model a reset cue yourself before the session. Show athletes what it looks like: mistake → physical reset → verbal cue → forward.

During

When you see a reset cue used genuinely, stop and name it: "Did everyone see that? That's a leadership reset. That's what we're building." Create positive reinforcement for the behaviour you want to see.

After

Confirm app complete. Reflection prompt. Ask each athlete to demonstrate their reset cue at the end of the session.

Q4 · W5
Giving and Receiving Feedback
35 XP
What to Notice

Who receives feedback defensively. An athlete who becomes defensive when given feedback is telling you something about their relationship with growth. Notice it, but do not call it out in front of the group. A private conversation: "I noticed you pulled back when [name] gave you feedback. What was happening for you?"

Before

Pair athletes deliberately. Mix ability and personality. Brief athletes on what specific feedback sounds like: "I noticed..." not "You should..."

During

Circulate and listen to feedback exchanges. Intervene if feedback is vague ("good job") -- prompt: "What specifically did you notice?" Do not let the session end without every athlete having given and received at least one specific piece of feedback.

After

Confirm app complete. Reflection prompt. This is a rich reflection week -- allow extra time.

Q4 · W6
Leading in Competition
35 XP
What to Notice

Who steps up under pressure and who disappears. Both responses reveal character. The athlete who disappears is not a poor leader -- they are a leader in development. The conversation afterwards is more important than the competition itself.

Before

Brief athletes clearly on the leadership mission before the competition starts. "Today, we are not measuring the score. We are measuring leadership. Who lifts their team when it's hard?"

During

Watch for leadership moments -- specific words, reset responses, physical signals of encouragement. Note 2-3 examples per athlete to use in the post-competition debrief.

After

Run the team reflection as described in the session challenge. Then confirm app complete and reflection prompt.

Q4 · W7
My Leadership Style
35 XP
What to Notice

Authenticity vs. performance. Some athletes will try to be the leader they think they should be rather than the one they naturally are. Watch for the athlete who is clearly uncomfortable in a vocal role but trying anyway -- this is the lead-by-example or encourager archetype. Name what you see: "I notice you lead differently when you are not performing -- what is most natural for you?"

Before

Plan the rotation order deliberately. Put athletes in leadership roles that challenge their comfort zone -- but brief each one privately first so they are not surprised.

During

After each rotation, give each athlete one piece of specific feedback on their leadership. Not general encouragement -- an observation: "When you gave the instructions just then, you lost two athletes in the middle. What would you do differently?"

After

Confirm app complete. Preview Week 8 -- tell athletes the Legacy Athlete conversation is next week and to think about what they want to say. Reflection prompt.

Q4 · W8 👑 Legacy Athlete
The Athlete I Am Becoming
135 XP
What to Notice

Growth from Week 1 of Quest I -- not just this Quest. The athletes in front of you are not the same athletes who began Level 1. Who leads differently? Who communicates differently? Who handles mistakes differently? These are the observations you name specifically in the Legacy conversation.

Before

Complete your Legacy conversation preparation for each athlete (see Section 2b above). Prepare the crown sticker for each athlete. Have the coach login ready to confirm the Legacy Athlete badge.

During

Run Week 8 session as normal. At the end, pause the group. Acknowledge that this is the Level 1 capstone. Run Legacy conversations individually or with the full group depending on your group's culture. Make the badge presentation ceremonial.

After

Award Legacy Athlete where criteria met -- the most significant sticker ritual of Level 1. Confirm badge in app. Tell each athlete one thing you will remember about coaching them through Level 1.
