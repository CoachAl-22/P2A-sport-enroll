IGNITE YOUR ENGINE

Realm of Discovery · The Explorer

Athletic ability is not something you are born with -- it is built. This is where your journey begins. Discover your starting point, train every physical quality, and ignite the engine that will carry you through every Quest ahead.

What This Module Is About

Module 1 builds the physical foundation -- speed, power, coordination, endurance, and movement quality. Every elite athlete started exactly where you are now.

You will discover your starting point at Week 1, train across all five physical qualities over 8 weeks, and complete a coach-assessed capstone at Week 8. This is not a test -- it is a starting point. Everything measured here becomes the baseline you prove growth against.

The lesson of the Explorer: You cannot improve what you cannot measure. Know your engine first -- then build it.

Q1 · Week 1
Know Your Engine
The Explorer's journey begins. Not a test -- a starting point. Everything from here is measured against today.
30 XP
Concept
What is Athletic Ability -- and Why Does it Matter?
Athletic ability is not something you are born with -- it is built. Key parts: Speed, Power, Coordination, Endurance, Movement Quality. Every elite athlete started exactly where you are now.
Do
Baseline Movement Assessment
Coach observes run, jump, throw, and change of direction.
  • Not a test -- a starting point
  • Ask coach: "What is one thing I do well, and one to improve?"
  • Keep notes to compare at Week 8
Reflect
Your Starting Point
"What does being an athlete mean to me?" Think about what kind of athlete you want to become -- not just what you can do now, but who you want to be.
Q1 · Week 2
Move Well
The Explorer discovers that quality of movement is the foundation everything else is built on.
25 XP
Concept
Quality First, Speed Second
Good movement is the foundation everything else is built on. Skipping, jumping, and running require coordinated patterns. "Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast. Quality first, speed second." -- P2A coaching principle.
Do
Agility Circuit + Out-and-Back Run
5-station agility circuit (ladder, weaving cones, hurdles, dodge and weave, skater hops), then steady out-and-back run matching pace each way.
  • Pick ONE cue to apply across every station
  • Focus on quality movement, not speed
Reflect
What Was Hardest
"Which movement felt hardest today -- and why do you think that is?" Was it coordination? Did fatigue break your technique? Did a specific station catch you off guard?
Q1 · Week 3
Speed
The Explorer learns that speed is not just natural gift -- it is technique, applied fast.
25 XP
Concept
Fast Starts, Arms, and Reaction
Speed comes from: powerful push-off, fast arm drive, forward lean from ankles. Arms control legs. First 10m is where races are won. Cue: "Head still, short fast arms, fast feet."
Do
Fast Starts + Partner Chase
Practise fast starts from different positions (standing, crouching, 3-point). Then Partner Chase -- one person starts 1m ahead, other must tag them before 10m mark.
  • Focus on the first 3 steps -- explosion, not stride
Reflect
When You Felt Fastest
"When did you feel fastest today -- and what were you doing differently in that moment?" Arm drive? More relaxed? Different start position? One rep where everything clicked?
Q1 · Week 4
Power
The Explorer discovers the difference between being strong and being explosive.
50 XP
⚡ Power Up · +25 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Jump, Bound, and Throw -- Explosive Power
Power = strength applied quickly. Two types trained: lower body (jumping/bounding) and upper body (throwing). Cues: "Jet wings on take-off. Reach for the sky. Motorbike landing."
Do
Bounding Hoops + Hop-Step-Jump
Bounding hoops -- bound through a line of hoops for distance. Hop-step-jump -- hop over hurdle, bound to dot, land in hoop.
  • Drive arms hard from push-off
  • Soft landings = skilled, not weak
Reflect
Where You Felt Power
"Where in your body did you feel the most power today -- and what helped you generate it?" Arms, legs, core? Did arm drive change your jump height? Did knee lift change bounding distance?
Q1 · Week 5
Hurdles & Relay
The Explorer learns that sport is more than solo performance -- it is timing, trust, and team.
25 XP
Concept
Rhythm, Baton Changes, and Running as a Team
Hurdle = attack at speed with controlled step-over; lead leg clears, trail leg whips through. Relay won or lost in the changeover zone. Cues: "Toes up, knees up, use arms. Arm straight back, call to run, go!"
Do
Hurdle Relay -- Team Best Time
Race over two hurdle lines, then full relay with baton changes. Goal: improve team time by running better technique, not just harder.
  • Incoming runner calls "hand"
  • Outgoing extends arm straight back and accelerates immediately
Reflect
Hurdles or Relay
"What was harder -- the hurdles or the relay exchange -- and what did you do to get better at it?" Identify one specific moment you improved. What changed -- a cue, a focus shift, or something a teammate said?
Q1 · Week 6
Endurance
The Explorer discovers that mental toughness is as much a physical quality as speed or power.
25 XP
Concept
Running at Your Own Pace
Aerobic base is built by sustained moderate effort -- not flat out. If you can't hold a short conversation, you're too fast. When it gets hard, focus on the next 30 seconds, not the whole remaining time. Cue: "Relaxed shoulders, steady breathing, head still."
Do
Age Endurance Challenge + Curve Running
Run continuously for half your age in minutes without stopping (e.g. age 12 = 6 minutes). Every complete minute without walking = 10 points.
  • Then curve running -- practise hugging the inside of a 50m curve
Reflect
Keep Going
"What was hardest about the endurance challenge -- and what strategy did you use to keep going?" Boredom, pain, or urge to walk? What did you tell yourself? Did early pacing help at the end?
Q1 · Week 7
Putting it Together
The Explorer realises how much more capable they are than at Week 1 -- all physical qualities working as one.
25 XP
Concept
Speed + Power + Coordination + Endurance in One Session
Skipping = coordination + power. Plyometrics (pogos, squat jumps) = explosive power. Strength exercises = foundation. Circular relay = speed while tired. Throw accuracy = brain-body skill under fatigue. Notice how much more capable you are than Week 1.
Do
Fitness Circuit + Circular Relay + Throw for Accuracy
Four areas: skipping/plyometrics, strength exercises (push-ups, lunges, squats, planks), circular relay, overarm throw for accuracy.
  • Set one personal target before starting
  • Coach high-5 at changeover
Reflect
How Far You've Come
"What has improved most since Week 1 -- and how do you know?" Identify a specific moment today where you were better than when you started. What was different -- in your body, technique, or mindset?
Q1 · Week 8 · Capstone
My Athletic Highlight Reel
The Explorer's journey is complete. Week 1 was the starting point. Week 8 is proof of progress.
85 XP
🏆 Module 1 Master · +50 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Looking Back to See How Far You Have Come
Week 1 = starting point. Week 8 = measure of progress. Athletic development is not a straight line -- some weeks were harder. Every session, every reflection, every challenge counts. After today, Module 2 -- Skill -- begins.
Do
Coach Assessment + Personal Best Attempt
Coach compares movement quality to Week 1 baseline. Athlete chooses personal best challenge:
  • Sprint relay, OR
  • Squat jump, OR
  • Tennis Ball Target
  • Coach awards Module 1 Master badge upon completion
Reflect
Who You Became
"What am I most proud of from Module 1 -- and what will I do differently in Module 2?" Not about what you achieved. About who you became. What habits will you carry forward? What will you do better?
