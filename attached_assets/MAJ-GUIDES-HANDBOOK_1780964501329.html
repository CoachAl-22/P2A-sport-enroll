A Word From Coach Al

My Athletic Journey was built on one belief: every athlete has potential. Not just the fastest, the strongest, or the most naturally gifted. Every single one of them.

Your job as a guide is to hold that belief on behalf of the athlete — especially on the days they can't hold it themselves. You are not here to evaluate their talent. You are here to develop their capability, build their confidence, and help them discover who they are capable of becoming.

This handbook is your reference. Read the mindset section and understand it thoroughly. Use the session cards every week. The more you live this philosophy, the better the athletes in your care will become.

Thank you for being part of this journey. It matters more than you know.

The Hero's Journey
The Three Roles
⚔️
The Athlete
The Hero
The athlete walks the path. Faces the challenges. Earns the growth. Every decision, every rep, every reflection belongs to them. The hero does the work.
🧭
The Coach
The Guide
The guide provides wisdom, asks questions, and helps the athlete discover their own potential. The guide does not carry the hero — the guide points the way. This is your role.
🤝
The Parent
The Ally
The ally provides encouragement, support, and belief. They walk beside the hero. Your relationship with parents should reinforce this role — never replace the hero's agency.
💡
The most common mistake: coaches who become the hero of someone else's story. When a coach takes over, directs every move, and gives every answer — the athlete stops owning their development. The guide's power comes from restraint. Ask more. Tell less.
The Mindset Shift
Guide vs. Instructor
✓  The Guide Does This
Asks "What did you notice about that rep?" instead of immediately correcting
Lets the athlete struggle productively before stepping in
Names the effort and process: "That took real focus"
Connects today's session to the bigger journey
Asks the reflection prompt and genuinely listens to the answer
Withholds a badge and explains clearly what is needed to earn it
Notices when an athlete is struggling beyond the physical
✗  The Instructor Does This
Immediately corrects every error without asking first
Rescues the athlete from productive struggle too early
Praises outcomes only: "That was fast" — not the effort behind it
Treats every session as isolated — no reference to the arc
Rushes through or skips the reflection step
Awards badges to avoid difficult conversations
Only coaches the physical — misses the whole athlete
What Great Looks Like
8 Behaviours of a Great Guide
1
You know each athlete's name and their skill focus
Before the session starts, you know what each athlete chose in Week 1 and where they are in their development. Personalised attention is the multiplier.
2
You read the week's concept before coaching it
The athlete reads it too. If you haven't read it, you can't connect their session experience back to the concept — and that connection is what makes learning stick.
3
You use the reflection prompt — and you listen
The reflection is not a formality. It is the most important two minutes of the session. An athlete who can articulate what they learned has locked it in. Ask the prompt. Wait. Listen fully before responding.
4
You notice and name effort and growth specifically
Not "good job." Instead: "I noticed you slowed down and checked your technique on rep 15 — that's exactly the discipline that builds skill." Specific recognition is infinitely more powerful than generic praise.
5
You connect today to the bigger journey
"Remember what you said in Week 1 about your goal? Look at where you are now." Athletes who understand they are on a journey stay longer, try harder, and grow more.
6
You hold the standard on badges
A badge awarded without being earned is worse than no badge at all. It tells the athlete their effort doesn't matter. If they are not ready at Week 8, tell them clearly what they need and give them a path to earn it.
7
You make the sticker moment meaningful
The gold star sticker is not just a sticker. It is a ritual that marks a real achievement. Stop the session. Make it visible. Name what the athlete did to earn it. Let it land.
8
You coach the person, not just the movement
Athletic development is the vehicle. Confidence, character, and resilience are the destination. When an athlete is struggling emotionally — with a setback, a bad day, a loss of confidence — notice it. Address it. That is the most important coaching you will ever do.

Badge Weeks
Badge Assessment Protocol
🏅
How to Assess and Award a Badge
1
Know the standard before the session. Each badge week card has specific, observable criteria for "Earned" and "Not Yet." Read them before the session so you know exactly what you're looking for.
2
Observe during the Do phase. You are not just running the session — you are assessing. Watch specifically for the criteria listed. Make a mental note before the session ends.
3
If earned: Tell the athlete specifically what you observed. "I'm awarding your Rep Champion badge because I saw you slow down and self-correct on rep 12 — that's exactly the discipline this badge represents." Then go to the sticker ritual.
4
If not yet earned: Be honest and specific. "You're not there yet — here's exactly what I need to see: [one specific thing]. Let's get you to that standard." Do not award a badge to avoid a hard conversation. Set a clear path to earn it.
5
Confirm in the app. Go to the coach login, find the athlete, and confirm the badge award. This triggers the digital badge on their profile. Do this before the end of the session or immediately after.
The Moment That Matters
The Sticker Ritual
⭐
The sticker is a ritual, not an admin task. When done well, this is one of the most memorable moments in an athlete's session. The gold star sticker represents something genuinely earned — make it feel that way. Compatible with standard 19mm gold star or gold dot stickers from Officeworks.
⏸️
Stop the session
Do not hand the sticker over while something else is happening. Stop. Make it the only thing happening in that moment.
🗣️
Name what was earned
Tell the athlete — and ideally the group — exactly what they did to earn the badge. Specific. Real. Not "great effort." "You held your technique through 20 reps including when you were tired. That is what the Rep Champion badge is for."
⭐
Peel and hand the sticker to the athlete
You peel it, you hand it to them. Do not just leave it on the table. The handover is part of the ritual. Let them place it on their Quest Map.
👏
Let the group acknowledge it
A brief moment of group recognition builds culture. Other athletes watching a badge be earned understand what it takes — and want it too.
📱
Confirm in the app
The physical sticker and the digital badge should land together. Confirm in the coach login so the athlete sees their badge in the app at the same session.
Keeping Families Aligned
Parent Communication

Parents are the Ally. Keep them informed, frame progress in terms of the journey, and never speak about an athlete's development in ways that compare them to others. Short, specific, genuine communication builds trust.

Badge Earned
"[Name] earned their Rep Champion badge today. They showed real discipline — they slowed down and self-corrected under fatigue. Worth celebrating at home tonight."
Badge Not Yet Earned
"[Name] is close to their badge — they need one more thing: [specific]. We've given them a clear path. They know what to work on. Watch for it next session."
Athlete Struggling
"[Name] had a tough session today — which is actually a good sign. Struggle is where growth happens. We talked about it. They're on the right track. Give them a quiet word tonight."
Quest Complete
"[Name] completed Quest [I/II] today. This is a real milestone — they've worked through 8 weeks and demonstrated genuine improvement. Ask them to show you their Quest Map tonight."

Build the Foundations
Realm of Discovery  ·  The Explorer
Q1 · Week 1
Ignite Your Engine
The Explorer takes their first step. Today is where the story begins and the baseline is set.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Confidence and comfort level
Who engages immediately and who holds back? First sessions reveal a lot about an athlete's relationship with challenge. Note who needs extra encouragement to commit to a baseline rating.
Before Session
  • Ensure all athletes have MAJ app access
  • Have Hero's Journals available
  • Prepare a brief overview of the 8-week journey
⚡ During Session
Walk athletes through what Quest I is about before starting. Frame everything as exploration — there are no wrong answers today. Help each athlete choose a genuine baseline rating out of 10. This number matters for Week 8.
After Session
  • Confirm app sessions complete
  • Note each athlete's baseline rating
  • Reflection prompt: "Why did I join — what do I want to achieve?"
Q1 · Week 2
Speed & Power
The Explorer discovers what their body can do when strength and speed combine.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Who surprised themselves
Athletes who discover unexpected capacity in this session often become the most committed. Name it when you see it: "That surprised you, didn't it? That's what's possible."
Before Session
  • Set up speed/power stations
  • Plan to record any measurable outputs for future comparison
⚡ During Session
Connect concept to session: "Power is strength plus speed. Today you're going to find out what your combination looks like." Push for genuine effort. Celebrate hard attempts as much as good results.
After Session
  • Confirm app sessions complete
  • Reflection: "What surprised you about how fast or powerful you can move?"
Q1 · Week 3
Coordination
The Explorer learns that the mind-body connection is trainable — not fixed.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Frustration with struggle
Coordination challenges expose athletes who believe ability is fixed ("I'm just not coordinated"). Watch for frustration. Interrupt it: "This is supposed to be hard. Your brain is literally building new pathways right now."
Before Session
  • Prepare coordination drills suited to age/ability
  • Have progressions ready — easier and harder versions
⚡ During Session
Explicitly link this to the Week 2 concept: skill is built through repetition. Today's struggle is today's growth. Ask athletes to rate their coordination at the start and end of the drill — they often improve within a single session.
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "What coordination challenge did I struggle with — and what will help me improve it?"
Q1 · Week 4
Endurance
The Explorer discovers that effort is a choice — and that choosing to keep going changes you.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Who chooses to stop early
Endurance sessions are character sessions. Who finds an extra gear and who opts out? Neither response is permanent — but both are worth a private conversation. This is coaching, not just fitness.
Before Session
  • Set clear, achievable targets for all ability levels
  • Warm-up that builds progressively
⚡ During Session
Give athletes something to aim at — not just "keep going." Use specific targets: "30 more seconds," "10 more metres." Acknowledge athletes who push past their comfort zone publicly and specifically.
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "When did I want to stop — and what made me keep going?"
Q1 · Week 5
Balance & Body Control
The Explorer learns that control is the foundation of all athletic expression.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Control vs. speed trade-off
Athletes who rush through balance drills to look capable. The lesson here is the opposite of hustle — slowing down reveals more. Name it: "The goal is control, not speed. If you're wobbling, slow down."
Before Session
  • Set up balance stations
  • Have progressions for athletes who master the base quickly
⚡ During Session
Slow everything down. Ask athletes to close their eyes at certain points — this removes visual compensation and forces true proprioceptive balance. Connect to sport: "Every time you change direction, this is what you're relying on."
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "What does it feel like when your body is in balance — and when does it fall apart?"
Q1 · Week 6
Reaction Time
The Explorer trains their brain — because in sport, the fastest decision is often the one that matters.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Anticipation vs. reaction
Some athletes are fast because they read the situation early. Others are fast because they are physically explosive. Both are valuable — help each athlete understand which is their strength and how to develop the other.
Before Session
  • Prepare reactive drills — audio and visual cues
  • Vary the signal type to test true reaction (not anticipation)
⚡ During Session
Vary timing so athletes can't predict the signal. Let them experience the difference between reacting and guessing. Connect to their sport: "When does reaction speed matter most in [their sport]?"
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "What helped you react faster today — and what slowed you down?"
Q1 · Week 7
Teamwork & Communication
The Explorer discovers that the best athletes make everyone around them better.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Who communicates — and how
Leadership often appears here first. Who organises, encourages, and communicates clearly under pressure? Note it. These athletes are your Quest IV candidates. Tell them what you saw.
Before Session
  • Design groups deliberately — mix ability and personality
  • Brief athletes: today is about making the person next to you better
⚡ During Session
Pause the game to spotlight specific moments of communication and support. Make the invisible visible: "Did you see what just happened? [Name] called the pass before anyone else knew it was coming. That's communication."
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Preview Week 8 — tell athletes what the showcase looks like
  • Reflection: "How did working with others change your performance?"
Q1 · Week 8 🏅 Foundation Builder Badge
Quest I Showcase
The Explorer proves what they have built. The Guide assesses, acknowledges, and awards.
85 XP
👁 What to Notice
Growth from Week 1
Compare each athlete to their Week 1 baseline — not to other athletes. Name the distance they have travelled. Even athletes who improved modestly deserve to hear their progress described specifically.
Before Session
  • Review each athlete's Week 1 baseline rating
  • Prepare gold star stickers
  • Have app coach login ready to confirm badges
⚡ During Session
Run the showcase as a structured demonstration. Each athlete performs, you assess against criteria, then you deliver your coaching feedback. Be specific about the improvement. This is the most important coaching conversation of the Quest.
After Session
  • Confirm Foundation Builder badge in app for each athlete
  • Complete the sticker ritual for every badge awarded
  • Tell each athlete one thing to carry into Quest II
✓ Foundation Builder — Earned
Demonstrates improved movement quality from Week 1 baseline
Can articulate one thing they have learned about their body
Has completed Learn, Do, and Reflect across the Quest
Shows up and engages fully at the Showcase
✗ Not Yet — What Is Needed
No measurable improvement from Week 1 (explore why before deciding)
Athlete cannot reflect on what they have learned
Missed more than 2 sessions without making them up
Quest II
⚔️
Sharpen the Tools
Realm of Practice  ·  The Apprentice
Q2 · Week 1
What Is Skill?
The Apprentice chooses their weapon. This decision defines the next eight weeks for both athlete and guide.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Are they choosing for the right reason?
Athletes sometimes choose skills they are already good at (safe) rather than the skill that would most improve them. Gently challenge this: "Is this the skill that will make the biggest difference to you in your sport?"
Before Session
  • Prepare skill options list
  • Have Hero's Journals ready for baseline ratings
  • Know each athlete well enough to guide their choice
⚡ During Session
Take time with skill selection — do not rush it. Once chosen, agree on what success looks like at Week 8 with each athlete. Be specific: not "I want to be better" but "I want to complete 15 clean reps under fatigue." Record the Week 1 skill rating carefully — this is the comparison point for Week 8.
After Session
  • Record each athlete's chosen skill and Week 1 rating
  • Confirm app sessions complete
  • Reflection: "What skill do I most want to improve — and why does it matter to me?"
Q2 · Week 2 🔁 Rep Champion Badge
Repetition
The forge begins. The guide watches not for speed or perfection — but for discipline and self-correction.
60 XP
👁 What to Notice
Self-correction without prompting
The Rep Champion badge is not for the best technique — it is for the most disciplined process. Watch for athletes who notice their own errors and slow down to fix them without being told. That is the behaviour to reward.
Before Session
  • Remind yourself of each athlete's chosen skill
  • Set up space for individual skill practice
  • Prepare the assessment criteria (see below)
⚡ During Session
Each athlete completes 20 reps of their chosen skill. Your job during the Do phase is to observe and note — not to correct continuously. Let them work through the reps. Step in between sets, not during. Ask: "Rate the quality of that set out of 10 — what would make it a 10?"
After Session
  • Award Rep Champion where criteria are met — sticker ritual
  • Confirm badge in app
  • Reflection: "What did you notice about your movement after 20 focused reps?"
✓ Rep Champion — Earned
Completes 20 reps without rushing between reps
Self-corrects or pauses when technique breaks (at least once)
Rates quality honestly — shows self-awareness, not just optimism
Stays focused for the full 20 reps without disengaging
✗ Not Yet — What Is Needed
Rushes through reps without checking technique
No self-correction — repeats errors without noticing
Disengages or loses focus during the set
Path to earn: one more focused set with one observed self-correction
Q2 · Week 3
Footwork
The Apprentice learns the foundation of all athletic movement. Time recorded today is a data point — revisited at Week 7.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Heavy vs. light foot contact
Heavy footwork is almost always correctable through cue words. "Light," "quick," "silent" are more effective than technical instructions. Watch for athletes who immediately improve with a single cue — file that away for future sessions.
Before Session
  • Set up footwork ladder or cone pattern
  • Have a stopwatch ready
  • Plan the pattern — walk-through first
⚡ During Session
Run the time trial protocol as designed. Record each athlete's fastest clean time. Focus first on pattern accuracy, then on speed — athletes who rush the pattern learn nothing useful. Connect to their chosen skill: "Where does footwork show up when you're practising [their skill]?"
After Session
  • Record Week 3 times — compare at Week 7
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "How does footwork affect everything else you do as an athlete?"
Q2 · Week 4
Throw & Catch
The Apprentice learns to work with others — because skill developed in isolation must eventually perform in partnership.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Response to partner mistakes
How does each athlete respond when their partner drops a catch or throws a bad pass? Frustration, encouragement, silence — each is a coaching moment. Character shows up most clearly when things go wrong.
Before Session
  • Pair athletes intentionally — consider ability and personality
  • Choose throw/catch object suited to ability
⚡ During Session
Run the 20-catch challenge. Set a reset tone: when a drop happens, no frustration — just reset and go again. Debrief mid-session: "What is your partner doing to make it easier for you to catch?" This shifts focus from self to team.
After Session
  • Note longest streak per pair
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "What did your partner do that helped you — and what did you do to help them?"
Q2 · Week 5 💨 Agility Ace Badge
Agility
The Apprentice proves they can move with control at speed. Time recorded today — revisited at Week 7.
60 XP
👁 What to Notice
Deceleration quality
Most athletes lose time at direction changes because they cannot decelerate efficiently. Watch their body position on turns — low and controlled is fast. High and stiff is slow. A single cue here often produces immediate improvement.
Before Session
  • Set up agility course (consistent with Week 7 retest)
  • Stopwatch ready — record every attempt
  • Know the course pattern yourself — walk it first
⚡ During Session
Walk-through, 70% speed, then timed. Record the best clean time for each athlete. "Clean" means the pattern was followed correctly — disqualify shortcuts. Encourage athletes to focus on direction changes, not just flat speed. Give one cue per athlete: "Lower hips on the turn."
After Session
  • Record Week 5 times — compare at Week 7
  • Award Agility Ace where criteria met — sticker ritual
  • Confirm badge in app
  • Reflection: "What slowed you down — and what is your plan to fix it?"
✓ Agility Ace — Earned
Completes the full course pattern without shortcuts
Attempts at least 4 timed runs with genuine effort
Shows controlled deceleration on at least one direction change
Records a best time that reflects maximum effort
✗ Not Yet — What Is Needed
Cutting corners or not following the full pattern
Not giving full effort on timed attempts
Path to earn: one more full-effort timed attempt following the complete pattern
Q2 · Week 6
Skill Under Pressure
The Apprentice faces the true test — can the forged tool hold its edge when tired?
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
What breaks first under fatigue
Every athlete has a technical breakdown point that is specific to them. Note what falls apart — focus, setup position, follow-through, communication. This is diagnostic data for Week 8 coaching.
Before Session
  • Plan warm-up circuit that creates genuine (not excessive) fatigue
  • Have each athlete's Week 2 quality rating to hand
⚡ During Session
Run the fatigue circuit, then immediately move to 20 skill reps. Do not let athletes rest long enough to fully recover — the point is performing tired. Ask mid-set: "What is the first thing that breaks when you're tired? What are you doing to hold it together?"
After Session
  • Record quality rating — compare to Week 2
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection: "Was your skill better or worse when tired — and what did you do to keep going?"
Q2 · Week 7
Game Situation
The Apprentice enters the arena. Skills forged in practice meet the unpredictability of real sport.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Skill use under game pressure
Watch specifically for athletes who consciously apply their chosen skill in game moments — not just athleticism generally. Who is thinking? Who is playing on instinct? Both are valuable data for the Week 8 coaching conversation.
Before Session
  • Re-run footwork drill (Week 3 retest) — record times
  • Re-run agility course (Week 5 retest) — record times
  • Prepare game activity suited to group size
⚡ During Session
Pause the game at least twice to ask: "[Name] — did you use your skill just then? What happened?" Make the conscious application visible to the group. Compare Week 3 and Week 5 times to this week's retests — share the numbers with athletes. Progress is motivating.
After Session
  • Share footwork and agility improvement data with athletes
  • Preview Week 8 Showcase — tell them what to expect
  • Reflection: "When in the game did you use your skill well — and what made that moment possible?"
Q2 · Week 8 🏆 Module 2 Master Badge
Skill Showcase — Capstone
The Final Trial. Eight weeks of forging, tested in one session. The Guide's coaching is revealed in the athlete's performance.
85 XP
👁 What to Notice
Process, not just outcome
An athlete who improved significantly but performs nervously at the showcase still deserves the badge if the growth is real. An athlete who performs well today but showed no process discipline across 8 weeks does not. Assess the journey, not just today.
Before Session
  • Review each athlete's Week 1 skill rating
  • Recall 2-3 specific observations from across their 8 weeks
  • Prepare gold star stickers — one per badge
  • Coach login ready on device
⚡ During Session
Each athlete: warm-up for their skill, then 20 quality reps with your full attention. Ask: "What is the biggest technical improvement you have seen in yourself over 8 weeks?" Your coaching response to their answer is the most important feedback of the Quest. Be specific. Be honest. Be encouraging.
After Session
  • Award Module 2 Master to qualifying athletes
  • Complete the full sticker ritual for each badge
  • Confirm all badges in app
  • Tell each athlete one skill goal for Quest III
✓ Module 2 Master — Earned
Measurable skill improvement from Week 1 baseline (higher self-rating supported by your observation)
Demonstrates awareness of what has improved and why
Showed process discipline across the Quest (reps, reflection, engagement)
Can name one skill goal to carry forward
✗ Not Yet — What Is Needed
No observable improvement from baseline (explore why — attendance? Effort? Approach?)
Cannot articulate what has changed or what they worked on
Path to earn: one additional focused session demonstrating the specific improvement
