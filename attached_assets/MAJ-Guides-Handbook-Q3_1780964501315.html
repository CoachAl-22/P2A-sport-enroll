Section 1
The Quest III
Coaching Shift
A Word on Quest III

In Quest I and II you coached bodies -- movement, technique, footwork, skill execution. You watched what athletes did and gave feedback on what you saw.

Quest III is different. You are now coaching something you cannot directly observe. Focus, self-talk, breathing patterns, and response to pressure are internal. You infer them from behaviour: eye contact, reset speed, quality of attention after errors, what an athlete says to themselves out loud.

Your job in Quest III is to make the invisible visible. Name what you observe about each athlete's mental state. Ask questions that surface their internal experience. Coach the mind as deliberately as you coached the body.

The Guide's Principles
Four Principles for Quest III
🔍
Notice, Don't Assume
You cannot see an athlete's self-talk. Ask: "What did you say to yourself just then?" before assuming they were negative.
🔄
Name Mental Resets
When an athlete breathes deliberately or uses a cue word after a mistake, name it publicly. "Did you see that? That was a mental reset. That is the skill."
⚡
Normalise Nerves
Never respond to an athlete's nerves with "just relax." Instead: "Your body is getting ready. That's the signal. Use it." Normalising pressure is coaching.
🏆
Reward the Process
A bad performance with great mental process still gets named. "I noticed you used your breathing after that mistake and came back stronger. That's what this Quest is about."
The Mindset Shift
Guide vs. Instructor
✓  The Guide Does This
Asks "what were you thinking?" before correcting technique
Pauses before pressure activities for breathing
Names self-talk used out loud by athletes
Rewards resets as much as results
Holds space for honest reflection after mistakes
✗  The Instructor Does This
Jumps straight to technical correction after every mistake
Never addresses emotional or mental state
Treats nerves as weakness or unprofessionalism
Skips reflection as unimportant
Only coaches the physical -- misses the whole athlete
Your Commitment
The Guide's Code — Quest III Addition
I coach the mind as deliberately as the body.
I make the invisible visible -- I name mental states I observe.
I never mock an athlete's nerves -- I help them use them.
I reward the reset as much as the result.
I ask before I tell, especially after a mistake.
I create space for breathing before I create pressure.

Q3 · Week 1
What is Focus?
The first session of Quest III. The guide's job today is to create safety for athletes to engage with mental concepts.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
First-session energy toward mental concepts
Some athletes will engage immediately. Others will be sceptical ("we're not going to meditate, are we?"). Notice both -- the sceptics often become the biggest converts. Don't push; just run the session as designed.
Before Session
Tell athletes this Quest is different -- they will be training their minds, not just their bodies. No judgement on anyone's focus level. Today is about noticing what is already there.
⚡ During Session
Run the mindful warm-up exactly as designed -- 2 minutes, quiet, coach-led. After the session, ask: "Did anyone notice a moment when you were completely locked in? What were you doing?" Let several athletes answer. Name specific observations you made about individual athletes' focus.
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Note which athletes engaged most with the mental content
  • Reflection prompt: "What makes it hard for me to focus during training -- and when is my focus at its best?"
Q3 · Week 2
Breathing and Calm
The guide models the tool. Athletes who see the coach breathe deliberately will follow.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Willingness to try the breathing exercise
Some athletes will feel self-conscious. Normalise immediately: "Every high-performance athlete in the world does this." Watch for athletes who visibly relax after the exercise -- name it.
Before Session
Read the 4-7-8 technique and practise it before the session. Lead it smoothly without reading from notes.
⚡ During Session
Lead the 4-7-8 breathing before the hardest activity yourself -- model it, don't just instruct it. Ask after: "Did anyone notice a difference? What did it feel like?" Connect to sport: "Every elite athlete has a pre-performance breathing routine. You now have one too."
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection prompt out loud
  • Remind athletes: use this as a reset tool after mistakes this week, not just before activities
Q3 · Week 3
Self-Talk
The guide is listening this week -- to what athletes say to themselves out loud.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
What athletes say when they make a mistake
Negative language ("I'm so bad at this") is common and important. You will address it through the session, not by correcting individuals directly.
Before Session
Have your own cue words ready to share as examples. Bring 2-3 examples of self-talk moments you have personally observed in athletes (without naming the athlete).
⚡ During Session
When you hear negative self-talk, pause the session and address the group: "I just heard something that every athlete says at some point. Let's talk about what to do instead." At the halfway point, ask athletes to share their chosen cue word with a partner.
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection prompt out loud
  • This is one of the richest reflection weeks -- allow extra time
Q3 · Week 4 🌬️ Calm Under Pressure
Attention Control
The guide observes where attention actually goes -- and names it without judgement.
60 XP
👁 What to Notice
Where attention goes under pressure
Task focus is observable -- eyes locked on the activity, body language present. Outcome focus is also observable -- glancing around, asking what the score is, looking away during their turn. Name neutrally: "I noticed your eyes went to the scoreboard -- what were you thinking?"
Before Session
  • Review each athlete's primary focus tool choice from Week 1
  • Prepare to assess the Calm Under Pressure badge
⚡ During Session
Introduce task vs. outcome concept before the drill. During the drill, count silently how many times each athlete shifts to outcome focus. Give one individual cue per athlete. Use badge assessment criteria throughout.
After Session
  • Award Calm Under Pressure where criteria met -- sticker ritual
  • Confirm badge in app
  • Reflection prompt out loud

Q3 · Week 5
Pressure and Nerves
The guide helps athletes reframe nerves as a signal, not a threat.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
How each athlete shows nerves
Every athlete shows nerves differently -- some go quiet, some get louder, some rush, some freeze. By Week 5 you should know each athlete's nervous pattern. Name it privately if appropriate: "I noticed you go quiet before your turn -- that's nerves. Here's what to do with them."
Before Session
Design the mini-competition so pressure is genuine but not overwhelming. Achievable challenge -- managed pressure, not failure.
⚡ During Session
Before the competition, give athletes 5 minutes to design their pre-performance routine (breathing + cue word + physical reset). Have them share with a partner. Observe who uses their routine and who abandons it under real pressure.
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Reflection prompt out loud
  • Tell athletes: the routine they designed today is theirs for life
Q3 · Week 6 📊 Consistent Performer
Consistency
The guide models honest self-rating and coaches athletes away from people-pleasing.
60 XP
👁 What to Notice
Honest self-rating vs. people-pleasing
Athletes who rate themselves 10/10 on everything are not self-aware. Athletes whose ratings match what you observed are developing genuine self-awareness -- name it.
Before Session
Prepare a simple effort/focus log (two columns per activity: Effort/10, Focus/10). Have it ready to hand out or read from.
⚡ During Session
Introduce the log before the first activity. Rate one activity yourself and share your honest score -- model the process. Check in with each athlete at least once about their log. The coaching conversation about the log is the learning moment.
After Session
  • Award Consistent Performer where criteria met -- sticker ritual
  • Confirm badge in app
  • Ask: "What activity produced your best focus? Could you recreate those conditions?"
✓ Consistent Performer -- Earned
Log completed honestly (ratings vary -- flat 10s suggest dishonesty)
Athlete identifies their highest and lowest focus conditions
Shows genuine self-awareness about what affects their concentration
✗ Not Yet -- What Is Needed
Log filled without honest rating or coaching conversation
Cannot identify what changed their focus across the session
Path to earn: one honest post-session log debrief with coach
Q3 · Week 7
Focus in a Team
The guide spotlights team focus moments -- making the invisible visible for the whole group.
35 XP
👁 What to Notice
Who lifts team focus and who drains it
Some athletes are natural focus anchors. Others are focus drains. Name the positive behaviour publicly. Address the negative behaviour privately.
Before Session
  • Plan groups deliberately -- mix ability and personality
  • Brief athletes: "Today is about what happens when everyone focuses together"
⚡ During Session
Run the team relay with the shared focus cue protocol. Pause at least once to spotlight a team focus moment: "Stop. Did you see that? [Name] called the cue and three athletes immediately reset. That is what team focus looks like."
After Session
  • Confirm app complete
  • Preview Week 8 -- the capstone involves demonstrating all three tools
  • Reflection prompt out loud
Q3 · Week 8 🏆 Module 3 Master
My Focus Toolkit — Capstone
The guide's coaching across 8 weeks is revealed in how each athlete performs and reflects today.
85 XP
👁 What to Notice
Evidence of growth from Week 1
Who resets faster? Who uses deliberate breathing? Whose self-talk has changed? Who maintains task focus under pressure that would have derailed them 8 weeks ago? These are the observations you name specifically during the badge conversation.
Before Session
  • Review each athlete's Week 1 reflection
  • Recall 2-3 specific observations from across their 8 weeks
  • Prepare your badge conversation for each athlete individually
⚡ During Session
Before the session, ask each athlete to name their three tools and when they plan to use each. During activities, actively look for tool use -- every time you see it, name it immediately. At the end: individual badge conversation with each athlete. Be specific. Be honest. Be encouraging.
After Session
  • Award Module 3 Master where criteria met -- full sticker ritual
  • Confirm all badges in app
  • Tell each athlete one mental skill they carry into Quest IV
  • Tell them: Quest IV is about leading others
✓ Module 3 Master -- Earned
Uses all three tools deliberately in the session (breathing, self-talk, task focus)
Can name each tool and give a real example of using it during this Quest
Shows observable change in response to pressure compared to Week 1
✗ Not Yet -- What Is Needed
Cannot name three tools with genuine examples
No observable change in pressure response across 8 weeks
Path to earn: one additional focused session with each tool demonstrated under coach observation
