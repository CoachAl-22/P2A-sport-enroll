Q4 · Week 1
What is Leadership?
The Hero's final journey begins with the hardest question: who are you as a leader right now?
35 XP
Concept
Self-Leadership
Self-leadership = taking responsibility for your own effort, attitude, and growth without being told. You do not need a title to be a leader -- you lead through your actions.
  • Leadership is not about being loud or the best
  • It is about being consistent and trustworthy
Do
My Self-Leadership Commitment
Three areas: effort, attitude, reliability. Choose one to develop most over 8 weeks.
  • Write "This module I will lead myself better by..."
  • Share commitment with coach
  • Make one deliberate leadership action during session
Reflect
Your Starting Point
"What kind of leader am I right now -- and what kind of leader do I want to become?"
Q4 · Week 2
Being a Great Teammate
The Hero discovers that great leadership begins with noticing others.
65 XP
💬 Encourager · 30 XP · Coach Required
Concept
Teammates First
Great teammates encourage others -- especially when things go wrong. Being a great teammate = supportive AND demanding high standards.
  • How you respond to your own mistakes sets the tone for the whole team
  • Noticing and celebrating others' good work is a leadership act
Do
Encourage 3 Teammates
Identify 3 teammates to encourage. Find a specific moment each does something well and name it specifically -- not "good job", but "Great arm drive on that sprint".
  • Deliver encouragement in the moment
  • Earn the Encourager badge
Reflect
The Effect
"How did encouraging others change how you felt and performed in the session?"
Q4 · Week 3
Communication
The Hero learns that leadership is broadcast through every signal -- not just words.
35 XP
Concept
Signals Beyond Words
Communication in sport is more than words. Body language, eye contact, energy, and positioning all send messages.
  • Positive body language lifts everyone around you
  • Listening is as important as speaking
Do
Relay Communication Challenge
Every handover must include a clear verbal and physical signal -- no silent handovers.
  • Agree as team on handover signal (word + gesture)
  • Practise 5 times standing still
  • Silent handover = team runs a lap
  • Standard: clear communication every time
Reflect
Impact Check
"What did you do to communicate better with your team today -- and what impact did it have?"
Q4 · Week 4
Handling Mistakes
The Hero's response to a mistake in the next 10 seconds defines their leadership.
35 XP
Concept
The Reset
Your response to a mistake is more important than the mistake itself. A reset cue signals to your brain: that is done, I am moving on.
  • Accountability without self-punishment
  • Acknowledge, identify what to do differently, move on
Do
Reset Cue Practice
Build and practise your own reset cue: a physical action (clap, short breath out, tap thigh) + a word (Next, Reset, Done).
  • Practise 10 times before session
  • Use it every time you make a mistake during session
  • Tell coach your cue and demonstrate it at end
Reflect
Mistake Response
"How did you handle a mistake or frustration today -- and what would you do the same or differently next time?"
Q4 · Week 5
Giving and Receiving Feedback
The Hero learns that the best teams tell each other the truth -- with care.
35 XP
Concept
Feedback That Helps
Effective feedback: specific, timely, focused on the action not the person.
  • Feedback sandwich: positive + specific improvement + encouraging close
  • Asking for feedback is strength, not weakness
  • Teams that give and receive feedback well improve faster
Do
Feedback Partner Challenge
Pair with a teammate. Observe during one activity.
  • Deliver: "I noticed that you..., and it works because..."
  • Receive partner's feedback with openness
  • Ask a follow-up question
Reflect
Both Sides
"What was hard about giving feedback -- and what was hard about receiving it?"
Q4 · Week 6
Leading in Competition
The Hero stays steady when everyone else is looking for a signal.
35 XP
Concept
Steady Under Pressure
Steady leadership = same effort, same communication, same reset response regardless of score.
  • Panic is contagious. So is calm.
  • The leader who stays composed gives everyone else permission to do the same
  • Your response in the next 10 seconds defines your leadership
Do
Lift the Team Regardless of Score
Before: agree on one leadership behaviour to bring.
  • During: focus on leadership mission more than the score
  • When behind: find a way to lift energy
  • After (win or lose): lead team reflection -- "What did we do well? What do we build on?"
Reflect
Hard Moments
"What did you do to help your team when things were hard -- and how did they respond?"
Q4 · Week 7
My Leadership Style
The Hero discovers there is no one way to lead -- only the way that is authentically theirs.
35 XP
Concept
Your Authentic Style
Leadership styles: vocal/energetic · quiet/consistent · leading by example · leading through encouragement · leading through challenge.
  • Your most effective style = the one authentic to who you are
  • Common myth: leaders must be loud. Some of the best sport leaders are quiet and consistent
Do
Session Leadership Rotation
Different session leader for each rotation. When your turn: lead warm-up or activity setup, give clear instructions, encourage teammates.
  • After rotation: ask 2 teammates "How did I go? What could I do better?"
  • Observe others in their leadership turns
Reflect
Natural Style
"What style of leadership felt most natural for me today -- and what does that tell me about the leader I am becoming?"
